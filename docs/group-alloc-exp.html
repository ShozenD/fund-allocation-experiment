Rules:

Once an allocation is agreed to, we will calculate the absolute difference between the amount given to an organization by the group and the amount you donated to the organization earlier. We will add up the differences for all seven groups. This amount will be subtracted from the $20 you received at the start. The amount left over will be added to your earnings for this phase of the experiment. See the table below for an example.

In other words, your goal is to adopt an allocation that minimizes the difference between the group's allocation and your own as much as possible. In addition, you can earn or lose money by:

  • If you are selected as a group leader, you will receive $1.
  • If you select one of the allocations from your group (your own or somebody else's), that person will receive an additional $1.
  • If you decide to create a new budget, you will receive an additional $1.
  • If anybody's allocation is adopted by the group, that individual will receive $2.
  • If the group is unable to agree on an allocation after 30 minutes, everybody will lose $20 and this phase of the study will end.

You have been randomly chosen as group leader!


Leader Task:

Your job is to either select or create an allocation of $20 to the same five organizations you donated to previously. The allocation that you select or create will be put up for a vote by all of the members of the group. To help you with this task, you will be provided with all of the allocations members of your group proposed previously. You can either select an allocation from the ones provided to you (including your own), or you can create an entirely new one to put up for a vote. Other members of the group will also be provided with all of the allocations members proposed previously.
